About the Beta Epsilon Chapter

Our Purpose

Our Purpose: The Purpose of Kappa Delta Chi is to promote the values of Unity, Honesty, Integrity and Leadership among women at colleges and universities.

Chapter History

The Beta Epsilon Chapter was established on April 12, 2013. Ten strong women chartered Kappa Delta Chi Sorority, Inc. at Michigan State University. The group of dedicated women was able to overcome many hardships and reach their ultimate goal in becoming part of such great sisterhood. Our charter class consists of: Valeria Rios, Clarissa Calzada, Priscilla Lopez, Melissa Chavez, Adelene Exceus, Clara Varela, Cristal Echevarria, Tania Alonso, Juana Lopez and Widad Nasser. The sisterhood continues to grow and serve their wonderful campus.

National History

In 1987 four young women from the Rio Grande Valley had a vision while attending Texas Tech University in Lubbock, TX. Their vision was to create something so special and unique that it would leave a legacy for others who followed. They recognized the need to unify and promote leadership amongst Hispanic women at the university. That vision became Kappa Delta Chi. The Founders are Cynthia Garza-Fleitman, Nellie Flores-Ledesma, Irene Montoya and Melissa Montoya. With the strong foundations of service, Christianity and friendship, they created a sisterhood that 20 years later is stronger than ever. They sought to incorporate the principles of Unity, Honesty, Integrity, and Leadership into this organization. Due to the hard work and effort of the Founders and the Charter members, Kappa Delta Chi was recognized as an official organization at Texas Tech University on April 6, 1987.
